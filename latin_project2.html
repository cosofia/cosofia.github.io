Juno

Queen of the gods, married to Jupiter. Her children are Mars, Vulcan and Hebe. Goddess of women and marriage.

Vesta

Tends the hearth on Mount Olympus. Goddess of the home and hearth. Often personified by the fire of her temple.

Pluto

God of the underworld (from the 3-reign sovereignty) and of wealth. Married to Proserpina. Strange, is how Hades and Pluto are viewed in their respective cultures, with the Greeks seeing him as a violent abductor with few temples dedicated to him and the Romans seeing him as a loving husband.

Venus

Goddess of love and beauty. Married to Vulcan. Lover of Mars. Famed for her role in the Trojan war.

Apollo

God of the sun, music, poetry and prophecy. Son of Jupiter and Latona. He was the god of the Delphic Oracles.

Diana

Twin sister of Apollo. Goddess of the moon and hunting. Daugher of Jupiter and Latona. She was one of the virgin goddesses.

Mars

God of war, second most important Olympian. His festivals were held in October (begining the season of conquest and campaigning) and in March (named after him). Some said that he was the father of Romulus and Remus, so he is the father of the Roman people.

Minerva

Goddess of strategy and wisdom. She had a strange birth (coming out of Jupiter's head). She is a virgin goddess. The Bavarian Illuminati's symbol was the owl of Minerva.

Mercury

God of messengers, thieves, merchants and travelers. Guide of souls to the underworld. In some parts of the Roman Empire, he was associated with financial gain. He was a son of Jupiter and Maia.

Ceres

Goddess of harvest and agriculture. She was a central figure in the Eleusinian mysteries, a secret initiaton ritual done annually.

Bacchus

God of wine and rejuvenation. Had a strange birth, he was sown into Zeus's thighs. Has many wild myths and tales. He could bring people back to life and was ressurected himself.

Proserpina

Goddess of flowers and spring, and Queen of the Underworld. Daughter of Demeter and Jupiter. Was abducted by Pluto.

Hercules

Son of Jupiter and a human Alcmene, known for his intense trials of strength and courage. After his death, he became a god.

Cupid

God of desire and sexual attraction, married to Psyche. In the Metamorphoses he was very a important charcacter, dealing with his adventures. Son of Venus and Mars

Ianus (Janus)

God of beginnings, gates, transitions, time, duality and doorways. Had no special priests assigned to him but he was always invoked at the beginning of each ceremony
